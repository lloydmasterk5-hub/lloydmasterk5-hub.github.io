# Week 01 — Beginning of Independent Research Projects

## Overview

This week marks the beginning of my independent research log. The objective of this website is to document my long-term learning and experimentation in mathematics, physics, and astrophysics.

Two main projects are currently being developed: experimental rocket construction, and theoretical universe modeling.

---


## Project 1 — Paper Rocket Prototype (Saturn V 1/96)

A paper model of the Saturn V rocket is currently under construction.

The objective of this project is not only the physical model itself, but also a theoretical analysis of its **center of mass**.

**Once the model is completed, the rocket will be approximated as a set of thin cylindrical and conical shells. Using these approximations, the center of mass will be calculated theoretically later with a post about 
the histoty of space exploration**
---

## Project 2 — Synthetic Universe Modeling

A third project was initiated this week: the exploration of a hypothetical universe constructed from alternative physical axioms.

The goal is to explore how different fundamental assumptions about space, matter, and interactions could lead to different physical structures.

Future work will begin with defining a minimal set of logical and physical axioms from which the rest of the model universe could emerge.

## Closing Notes

This first week focused primarily on defining research directions and establishing the conceptual foundations of each project. Quantitative work and experiments will follow in the coming weeks as the models and prototypes develop further.
